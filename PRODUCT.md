# Product

## Register

product

## Users

VRCNT-Next serves VRChat users who participate in multilingual voice and chat sessions on Windows. They run the app beside a performance-intensive VR client and need microphone, speaker, translation, chatbox, and overlay workflows to remain understandable and responsive without leaving the session.

## Product Purpose

Make multilingual VRChat conversations easier through real-time transcription and translation, local AI support, cloud-provider fallback, readable language controls, and VR-aware output. Success means recognized speech appears promptly, delays are attributable to a visible stage, final output is accurate, and the app consumes only the resources necessary for the selected workflow.

## Brand Personality

Practical, focused, clear. The product should feel modern and capable while staying calm enough to run as a companion utility during live conversation.

## Anti-references

- A feature-bloated plugin platform with controls unrelated to translation or transcription.
- Opaque loading states that make a working pipeline look frozen.
- Decorative, high-motion interfaces that compete with VRChat or consume avoidable GPU resources.
- Novel interaction patterns that make familiar desktop settings and status controls harder to understand.

## Design Principles

- Show work immediately and identify the stage causing a delay.
- Keep the primary language, audio, engine, and activation controls readable at a glance.
- Protect the VR session: support performance mode and avoid unnecessary animation or rendering work.
- Treat every supported locale as a complete product surface, not an afterthought.
- Prefer familiar desktop behavior and explicit system state over ornamental novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

State must be conveyed with text or icons in addition to color. Text remains readable on narrow layouts, reduced-motion preferences are respected, and performance mode retains meaning without blur, glow, or animation. Keyboard focus and live status announcements must remain usable for blocking and asynchronous operations. The six supported interface locales must preserve schema and interpolation parity.
